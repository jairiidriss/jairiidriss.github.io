About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at LIRMM (Montpellier Laboratory of Informatics, Robotics, and Microelectronics), specializing in deep learning applications for environmental monitoring. My research focuses on developing multimodal AI approaches that combine vision, audio, and sensor fusion to address real-world challenges in biodiversity and sustainability.

I completed my Ph.D. at the University of Lille, where I developed intelligent air quality indicators using machine learning, with a focus on explainable AI and transfer learning. My work has been published in top-ranked international journals, and I am passionate about making AI accessible and impactful for environmental and health preservation.
